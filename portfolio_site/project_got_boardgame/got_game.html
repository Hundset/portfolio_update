Game of Thrones browser board game - First Semester Assignment 2020

Our assignment was to create an online board game based on the HBO tv-series; Game of Thrones. This presented a unique and in many ways exciting challenge, as everything I had worked on up til this point has been a lot more orthodox: CSS and JS for the purpose of “traditional” websites meant to inform and sell the user on a product and/or company.

The rules for the game were already decided: Choosing two player characters from a list of 10 in a “Snakes & ladders” style of game with 30 tiles from beginning to end, a 6 sided dice to generate movement, traps along the way to stall progress, a player getting another turn if they rolled a 6.

The background image is a piece of fan-art of the Iron Throne, creating immediate associations to the namesake of the source material. Despite the game's rules being inspired by snakes and ladders, I opted for a monopoly style layout in order to keep the throne central.

I primarily took inspiration from HBO's own sites; (viewer's guide and the fficially licensed browser game). The former's colour scheme was primarily composed of various shades of gray

Both in its advertisements and its front page the official browser game presents the throne: one side on fire, and the other frozen in ice - an allusion to the title of the book series - A Song of Ice and Fire.

Similarly two of my three primary colours be Blue and Red, with a lighter orange used for “highlights”, used as a complementary colour to the Blues but also to contrast with the darker backdrops.

I chose a less muted colour scheme despite the source material's tone and themes because the site emulates a board game, which are usually colourful to evoke a sense of playfulness and excitement.

Given that I have some talents/skill in drawing, I was convinced that making my own illustrations would be for the best, even if I'm not very experienced with colouring art. At this point in time I was fortunate enough to have a family member offer to colour my drawings for me, which I chose to accept. Over time we both agreed for the sake of their workflow and meeting the deadline that we would use illustrations they created from scratch - as they found this easier.

Aesthetically I'm decently happy with the final result, although information distribution might be a bit too constrained to the left side of the screen. I am most happy with the board itself, with the graphics used for the tile patterns and the icons, I tried to hit a sweet spot with recognizable detail that would remain visible when scaled down to the tiles.

Given my starting point at the onset of this assignment I'm happy that I managed to get as far as I did and this is certainly progress in my own development. While it revealed at the time that I still have a lot to learn about working in Javascript and writing more DRY and reusable code, I did a lot of learning on the way on this particular project.

It is for this reason that I choose to showcase this site as a part of my portfolio: the site's design, my work on the logo and icons, to catalogue my progress and to show my willingness to learn and self-reflect.
